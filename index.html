Part 1: Hybrid Image


My favorite result:


This is my favorite hybrid image result out of all the experiments. When you look close at the computer, you will see John Wick (Keanu Reeves) handsome as always, but when you step a few meters away, you will see that it's acually a picture of The Nun. In this example, I chose 7 for the cut-off frequency of low-pass filter, and 8 for the cut-off frequency of high-pass filter.

Throughout the experiment of using different cut-off frequencies as well as different pictures, I realized that whether the hybridization of image will success depends highly on whether the two input images are already roughly similar to each other - for example, if the two input images are both portraits and whether people in the portraits have the same pose.

Part 2: Image Enhancement


Contrast Enhancement


This is a picture of Golden Bridge that I took a year ago. As you can see on the left, the original image looks misty and has a poor contrast, while the image on the right after contrast enhancement looks clearer and has a strong third dimension. For the implementation of contrast enhancement, I chose histogram equalization as it could redistribute pixel values on a linear level.


Color Enhancement


This is a picture that I took in Disney World this summer. For color enhancement, I first converted the color space to HSV and separated saturation from hue and value. Then I normalized saturation by dividing each pixel's saturation by 255, and used a gamma correction with γ = 0.6. As you can see on the right, image after color enhancement looks more vivid and has a brighter color than the original image.


Color Shift


This is a picture that I took on the plane when I was flying back to China last summer. For color shifting, I first converted the color space to LAB and separated each channel. In order to achieve a more red image, I added 10 to a(the difference between green and red), and in order to achieve a less yellow image, I subtracted 10 from b(the difference between blue and yellow). As you can see in the middle is the reddish image, and on the right is the less-yellow image.
